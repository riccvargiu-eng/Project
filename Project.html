Metal emerged in the late 1960s from psychedelic and hard rock, with bands like Black Sabbath and Led Zeppelin introducing distorted guitars, heavy riffs, and dark themes. In the 1980s, it diversified into subgenres such as classic heavy metal (Iron Maiden, Judas Priest), thrash (Metallica, Slayer), glam (Mötley Crüe), and early forms of death and black metal. In the 1990s, nu metal (Korn) and alternative/progressive metal (Tool, Dream Theater) appeared, while the 2000s saw the rise of metalcore, deathcore, and revivals of power and classic metal. Common traits across the genre include distorted guitars, aggressive rhythms, powerful vocals, and lyrics ranging from rebellion to fantasy or social critique.

"Metal isn't necessarily aggressive. There's metal that's contemplative, there's metal that's sad, and there's metal that's exuberant.

No genre is limited in what it can express." - John Darnielle.
