Linkin Park was formed in Agoura Hills, California in 1996 and quickly became a defining act of the early 2000s nu‑metal and alternative rock scene. Their debut album, Hybrid Theory (2000), fused heavy guitar riffs, rap, electronic elements, and melodic choruses, achieving massive global success. As their career progressed, the band broadened their sound with albums such as Meteora (2003) and Minutes to Midnight (2007), exploring alternative rock, electronica, and pop‑influenced textures. After the tragic passing of lead vocalist Chester Bennington in 2017, the band entered a hiatus but announced a new chapter in 2024.

“I’m strong on the outside, not all the way through. I’ve never been perfect, but neither have you.” - Chester Bennington
