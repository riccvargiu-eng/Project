Opeth was formed in Stockholm, Sweden in 1990 and began as a death‑metal outfit, but under frontman Mikael Åkerfeldt they evolved into one of the most inventive progressive‑metal bands in existence. Their early albums, such as Orchid (1995) and My Arms, Your Hearse (1998), blended extreme metal techniques with acoustic passages and clean vocals. With Blackwater Park (2001), they achieved a commercial and artistic breakthrough, touring worldwide. Over time, Opeth continued to experiment, incorporating jazz, folk, and classic‑rock elements while retaining their metal roots.

“The essence of Opeth isn’t death‑metal vocals. The essence of Opeth is change.” — Mikael Åkerfeldt
